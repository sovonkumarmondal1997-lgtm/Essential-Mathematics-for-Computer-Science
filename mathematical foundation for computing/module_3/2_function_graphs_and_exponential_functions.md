# Function Graphs, Linear Functions, Quadratic Functions and Exponential Functions

---

# 1. Function Plotting / Graphing

## Definition

Function plotting (or graphing) means drawing a picture of a function on a coordinate plane.

Instead of only looking at equations, we visualize how inputs and outputs are related.

---

## Basic Idea

Step 1: Choose input values (x)

Step 2: Calculate outputs (y)

Step 3: Plot points (x,y)

Step 4: Connect points

---

## Example

Function:

f(x)=x+2

Table:

| x | y=x+2 |
|---|---|
| 0 | 2 |
| 1 | 3 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 5 |

Points:

(0,2)

(1,3)

(2,4)

(3,5)

When connected, they form a straight line.

---

## Real-Life Example

Hours Worked → Salary Earned

| Hours | Salary |
|---------|---------|
| 1 | ₹100 |
| 2 | ₹200 |
| 3 | ₹300 |

Plotting these points shows how salary increases with hours.

---

# 2. Linear Function

## Definition

A Linear Function has the form:

f(x)=ax+b

where:

a = slope (gradient)

b = y-intercept

Its graph is always a straight line.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=2x+1

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 3 |
| 2 | 5 |
| 3 | 7 |

Straight line graph.

---

## Example 2

f(x)=x−4

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | -4 |
| 2 | -2 |
| 4 | 0 |

Straight line graph.

---

## Real-Life Example

Taxi Fare:

Fare = Base Fare + Distance Charge

Fare = 50 + 10x

This is a linear function.

---

# 3. Y-Intercept

## Definition

The Y-Intercept is where the graph crosses the y-axis.

This happens when:

x=0

Point:

(0,b)

---

## Example 1

f(x)=2x+3

Put x=0:

f(0)=3

Y-intercept:

(0,3)

---

## Example 2

f(x)=5x−2

f(0)=-2

Y-intercept:

(0,-2)

---

## Real-Life Example

Taxi Fare:

Fare = 50 + 10x

Even if distance = 0,

Fare = ₹50

Y-intercept = ₹50

---

# 4. Gradient / Slope (a)

## Definition

Slope tells us:

- How steep a line is
- Whether it goes upward or downward

---

## Formula

Slope = Change in y / Change in x

---

## Example 1

f(x)=2x

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 4 |

Slope:

(4−2)/(2−1)=2

---

## Example 2

f(x)=−3x

Slope = -3

Line moves downward.

---

## Interpretation

Positive slope → Increasing line

Negative slope → Decreasing line

Zero slope → Horizontal line

---

## Real-Life Example

Speed:

Distance = 60 × Time

Slope = 60

Car travels 60 km every hour.

---

# 5. Increasing Function

## Definition

A function is increasing when larger inputs produce larger outputs.

As x increases,

f(x) increases.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=x+2

| x | f(x) |
|---|---|
| 1 | 3 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 5 |

Output keeps increasing.

---

## Example 2

f(x)=2x

1 → 2

2 → 4

3 → 6

Increasing function.

---

## Real-Life Example

Hours Studied → Marks

More study generally leads to better marks.

---

# 6. Decreasing Function

## Definition

A function is decreasing when larger inputs produce smaller outputs.

As x increases,

f(x) decreases.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=-x

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 1 | -1 |
| 2 | -2 |
| 3 | -3 |

Outputs decrease.

---

## Example 2

f(x)=10−x

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 1 | 9 |
| 2 | 8 |
| 3 | 7 |

Decreasing function.

---

## Real-Life Example

Fuel Remaining vs Distance Travelled

More distance travelled means less fuel left.

---

# 7. Quadratic Function

## Definition

A quadratic function has the form:

f(x)=ax²+bx+c

where:

a ≠ 0

Its graph is a parabola.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=x²

| x | y |
|---|---|
| -2 | 4 |
| -1 | 1 |
| 0 | 0 |
| 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 4 |

---

## Example 2

f(x)=x²+1

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 5 |

---

## Real-Life Example

Height of a thrown ball often follows a quadratic function.

---

# 8. Parabola

## Definition

A parabola is the curved shape produced by a quadratic function.

---

## Upward Parabola

f(x)=x²

Shape:

∪

---

## Downward Parabola

f(x)=−x²

Shape:

∩

---

## Examples

x²

x²+2

2x²−1

All create parabolas.

---

## Real-Life Example

Water from a fountain forms a parabola.

---

# 9. Vertical Shift / Graph Translation

## Definition

Moving a graph up or down without changing its shape.

---

## Upward Shift

Original:

f(x)=x²

Shift up by 3:

f(x)=x²+3

Every point moves 3 units upward.

---

## Example

Original point:

(2,4)

After shift:

(2,7)

---

## Downward Shift

f(x)=x²−2

Every point moves down 2 units.

---

## Real-Life Example

Think of lifting an entire bridge upward without changing its shape.

---

# 10. Reflectional Symmetry

## Definition

A graph has reflectional symmetry if one side mirrors the other side.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=x²

Points:

(-2,4)

(2,4)

Mirror images.

---

## Example 2

f(x)=−x²

Also symmetric.

---

## Reflection Across X-Axis

x²

becomes

−x²

Graph flips upside down.

---

## Real-Life Example

Butterfly wings are symmetric.

---

# 11. Exponential Function

## Definition

An exponential function has the form:

f(x)=bˣ

where:

b>0

b≠1

The variable is in the exponent.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=2ˣ

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 8 |

---

## Example 2

f(x)=3ˣ

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 3 |
| 2 | 9 |

---

## Real-Life Example

Money growing with compound interest.

---

# 12. Laws of Exponents / Index Laws

## Definition

Rules used to simplify powers.

---

## Rule 1

aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ

Example:

2² × 2³

= 2⁵

= 32

---

## Rule 2

aᵐ / aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ

Example:

2⁵ / 2²

= 2³

= 8

---

## Rule 3

(aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ

Example:

(2²)³

= 2⁶

= 64

---

## Rule 4

a⁰ = 1

Example:

5⁰ = 1

100⁰ = 1

---

## Rule 5

a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ

Example:

2⁻³

=1/8

---

# 13. Exponential Growth

## Definition

Occurs when:

b>1

Outputs increase faster and faster.

---

## Example

f(x)=2ˣ

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 8 |
| 4 | 16 |

Growth becomes rapid.

---

## Real-Life Examples

- Compound interest
- Population growth
- Viral social media posts
- Spread of information

---

# 14. Y-Intercept of Exponential Functions

## Definition

All basic exponential functions pass through:

(0,1)

because:

b⁰=1

---

## Example 1

2⁰=1

Graph crosses at:

(0,1)

---

## Example 2

5⁰=1

Graph crosses at:

(0,1)

---

## Example 3

10⁰=1

Graph crosses at:

(0,1)

---

## Important Fact

Every basic exponential graph crosses the y-axis at 1.

---

# 15. Horizontal Asymptote

## Definition

A line that a graph gets closer and closer to but never touches.

---

## Example

f(x)=2ˣ

As x becomes very negative:

2ˣ approaches 0

but never reaches 0.

---

## Horizontal Asymptote

y=0

(the x-axis)

---

## Real-Life Example

A bouncing ball.

Each bounce becomes smaller.

It approaches the floor but mathematically never exactly reaches zero height.

---

# 16. Exponential Decay

## Definition

Occurs when:

0<b<1

Outputs become smaller and smaller.

---

## Example 1

f(x)=(1/2)ˣ

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 0.5 |
| 2 | 0.25 |
| 3 | 0.125 |

---

## Example 2

f(x)=(1/3)ˣ

| x | y |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 0.333 |
| 2 | 0.111 |

---

## Real-Life Examples

- Radioactive decay
- Battery discharge
- Medicine leaving the body
- Depreciation of assets
