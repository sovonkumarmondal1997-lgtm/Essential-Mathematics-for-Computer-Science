# 1. Two-Dimensional Plane (2D Space)

## What is a Two-Dimensional Plane?

A two-dimensional (2D) plane is a flat surface that has:

- Length (horizontal direction)
- Width (vertical direction)

It has **no depth or thickness**.

The easiest example is a piece of paper or graph paper.

### Examples

- Graph paper
- Chessboard
- Computer screen
- Map of a city

---

## Understanding with Graph Paper

Imagine graph paper:

```text
y
↑

5 | . . . . .
4 | . . . . .
3 | . . . . .
2 | . . . . .
1 | . . . . .
0 +-----------
   0 1 2 3 4 → x
```

Every point has two coordinates:

```text
(x, y)
```

Example:

```text
(3,2)
```

means:

- Move 3 units right
- Move 2 units up

---

## Real-Life Example

Imagine a treasure map.

```text
Treasure = (5,3)
```

To reach it:

- Walk 5 steps east
- Walk 3 steps north

You arrive at the treasure location.

# 2. Properties of Angles and Rotation

---

## What is an Angle?

An angle measures how much one line rotates away from another line.

It is measured in degrees (°).

---

## Total Rotation = 360°

If you spin completely around one point and return to your starting direction:

```text
Full Circle = 360°
```

### Example

Imagine standing and turning:

- Quarter turn = 90°
- Half turn = 180°
- Three-quarter turn = 270°
- Full turn = 360°

```text
      North
        ↑
West ← • → East
        ↓
      South
```

Returning to North after a complete spin means:

```text
360°
```

---

## Straight Line Angle = 180°

A straight line divides a full rotation into two equal halves.

```text
←────────────→
```

The angle formed is:

```text
180°
```

### Example

If you walk forward and then turn completely around:

```text
180°
```

You are now facing the opposite direction.

---

## Direction of Movement

In mathematics:

### Counter-Clockwise (Anticlockwise)

Positive direction (+)

```text
      ↑
      |
      |
←-----•-----→
      |
      |
      ↓
```

Rotating from right to up:

```text
+90°
```

---

### Clockwise

Negative direction (-)

Rotating from right to down:

```text
-90°
```

---

## Example

Clock hands move:

```text
Clockwise
```

But mathematicians usually measure angles:

```text
Counter-clockwise
```

as positive.

# 3. Perpendicular Lines and Right Angles

---

## Perpendicular Lines

Two lines are perpendicular if they meet and create equal angles.

The original 180° straight angle is split into two equal parts:

```text
180° ÷ 2 = 90°
```

---

### Example

```text
      |
      |
------|------
      |
      |
```

The lines intersect at:

```text
90°
```

---

## Right Angle

The angle formed by perpendicular lines is called a right angle.

```text
90°
```

Symbol:

```text
∟
```

---

## Real-Life Examples

### Wall and Floor

```text
Wall
 |
 |
 |
 |____ Floor
```

Angle:

```text
90°
```

---

### Corner of a Book

Every corner forms:

```text
90°
```

---

### Window Frame

Vertical and horizontal bars meet at:

```text
90°
```

# 4. Introduction to Triangles

---

## What is a Triangle?

A triangle is a shape formed by joining three straight lines.

It has:

- 3 sides
- 3 angles
- 3 vertices (corners)

---

### Example

```text
      A
     / \
    /   \
   /     \
  B-------C
```

Vertices:

```text
A, B, C
```

---

## Naming Convention

Angles use CAPITAL letters:

```text
A, B, C
```

Sides use lowercase letters opposite the angles.

```text
      A
     / \
   b/   \c
   /     \
  B---a---C
```

Notice:

- Side a is opposite angle A
- Side b is opposite angle B
- Side c is opposite angle C

---

# 5. Fundamental Laws and Formulas of Triangles

---

# Interior Angle Sum

For every triangle:

```text
A + B + C = 180°
```

---

## Example

Suppose:

```text
A = 50°
B = 60°
```

Find C:

```text
50 + 60 + C = 180

110 + C = 180

C = 70°
```

---

# Area of a Triangle

Formula:

```text
Area = ½ × Base × Height
```

---

## Example

```text
Base = 10 cm
Height = 6 cm
```

Area:

```text
= ½ × 10 × 6

= 30 cm²
```

---

## Visual

```text
      /|
     / |
    /  | Height
   /   |
  /____|
   Base
```

---

# Perimeter of a Triangle

Perimeter means total distance around the triangle.

Formula:

```text
Perimeter = a + b + c
```

---

## Example

```text
a = 5
b = 7
c = 8
```

Perimeter:

```text
5 + 7 + 8 = 20
```

units

---

# 6. Types of Triangles

---

# Isosceles Triangle

A triangle with:

- Two equal sides
- Two equal angles

---

## Example

```text
      A
     /\
    /  \
   /____\
  B      C
```

If:

```text
AB = AC
```

then:

```text
∠B = ∠C
```

---

## Real-Life Example

A house roof often forms an isosceles triangle.

---

# Equilateral Triangle

A triangle where all sides are equal.

```text
a = b = c
```

---

Since angle sum is 180°:

```text
180° ÷ 3 = 60°
```

Each angle is:

```text
60°
```

---

### Example

```text
      A
     /\
    /  \
   /____\
  B      C
```

All sides equal.

---

# Similar Triangles

Two triangles are similar if:

- Same angles
- Different sizes allowed

---

## Example

Small triangle:

```text
3,4,5
```

Large triangle:

```text
6,8,10
```

Every side doubled.

Shape remains identical.

Therefore:

```text
Similar
```

---

# Real-Life Example

A photo enlarged on a photocopier.

The picture becomes bigger but keeps the same shape.

# Using θ (Theta)

Instead of A, B, C, one important angle is often called:

```text
θ (theta)
```

---

Example:

```text
90°
+
θ
+
remaining angle
=
180°
```

Therefore:

```text
remaining angle = 90° − θ
```

Consider:

```text
        /|
       / |
      /  | Opposite (o)
   h /θ  |
    /____|
 Adjacent(a)
```

---

## Adjacent Side (a)

Next to θ

---

## Opposite Side (o)

Directly across from θ

---

## Hypotenuse (h)

Longest side.

Always opposite:

```text
90°
```

angle.

---

# Example

Suppose:

```text
θ = 30°
```

Then:

- Side beside θ = Adjacent
- Side across θ = Opposite
- Longest side = Hypotenuse

# 8. Pythagoras' Theorem

---

## Definition

For every right-angled triangle:

```text
h² = o² + a²
```

where:

- h = Hypotenuse
- o = Opposite
- a = Adjacent

---

## Meaning

The square of the longest side equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

---

# Famous 3-4-5 Triangle

```text
      |
    4 |\
      | \
      |  \ 5
      |   \
      |____\
         3
```

Check:

```text
5² = 3² + 4²

25 = 9 + 16

25 = 25
```

Correct.

---

# Example 2

Find the hypotenuse.

```text
Adjacent = 6
Opposite = 8
```

Using:

```text
h² = 6² + 8²

h² = 36 + 64

h² = 100

h = √100

h = 10
```

Answer:

```text
Hypotenuse = 10
```

---

# Example 3

Find a missing side.

```text
Hypotenuse = 13
Adjacent = 5
```

Using:

```text
13² = o² + 5²

169 = o² + 25

o² = 144

o = 12
```

Answer:

```text
Opposite Side = 12
```
