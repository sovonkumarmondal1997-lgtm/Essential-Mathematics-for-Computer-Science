# Circles, Pi, Accuracy, Degrees, and Radians

---

# 1. Defining a Circle (Origin and Radius)

## What is a Circle?

A circle is a perfectly round shape where every point on the edge is the same distance from the center.

---

## Origin (Center)

The **origin** (or center) is the point exactly in the middle of the circle.

```text
        *
     *     *
   *    O    *
     *     *
        *
```

Here:

```text
O = Origin (Center)
```

Every point on the edge is equally far from O.

---

## Radius (r)

The **radius** is a straight line drawn from the center to any point on the edge.

```text
      *
    / |
   /  |
  O---*
      r
```

Radius is usually represented by:

```text
r
```

---

## Why is Radius Important?

The radius completely determines the size of a circle.

If two circles have the same radius:

- They have the same size
- They have the same shape

---

## Example 1

Circle A:

```text
r = 5 cm
```

Circle B:

```text
r = 5 cm
```

Both circles are identical.

---

## Example 2

Circle A:

```text
r = 3 cm
```

Circle B:

```text
r = 10 cm
```

Circle B is much larger.

---

## Real-Life Examples

### Pizza

The center of the pizza:

```text
Origin
```

Distance from center to crust:

```text
Radius
```

---

### Bicycle Wheel

Hub:

```text
Origin
```

Spoke length:

```text
Radius
```

---

# 2. Circumference

## What is Circumference?

The circumference is the total distance around the outside edge of a circle.

It is the circle's version of a perimeter.

---

## Formula

```text
Circumference = 2πr
```

where:

- π ≈ 3.14159
- r = radius

---

## Example 1

Radius:

```text
r = 5 cm
```

Circumference:

```text
C = 2πr

C = 2 × π × 5

C = 10π

C ≈ 31.42 cm
```

---

## Example 2

Radius:

```text
r = 10 m
```

Circumference:

```text
C = 2π(10)

C = 20π

C ≈ 62.83 m
```

---

## Real-Life Example

Suppose a circular running track has:

```text
Radius = 50 m
```

One complete lap is:

```text
C = 2π(50)

≈ 314.16 m
```

---

# 3. The Number Pi (π) as an Irrational Number

## What is π?

Pi (π) is one of the most important numbers in mathematics.

It represents the relationship between:

```text
Circumference ÷ Diameter
```

For every circle:

```text
π ≈ 3.141592653589793...
```

---

## Irrational Number

An irrational number:

- Never ends
- Never repeats a pattern
- Cannot be written exactly as a fraction

---

## Examples of Irrational Numbers

```text
π = 3.141592653...
```

```text
√2 = 1.414213562...
```

```text
e = 2.718281828...
```

---

## Why π Cannot Be Written Exactly

Fractions repeat or terminate:

```text
1/2 = 0.5
```

```text
1/3 = 0.333333...
```

But π never repeats.

Therefore:

```text
π cannot be represented exactly as p/q
```

---

## Computational Limitation

Computers have limited memory.

Therefore:

```text
π cannot be stored exactly
```

Computers store only approximations.

Examples:

```text
3.14
```

```text
3.14159
```

```text
3.141592653589793
```

The more digits stored, the more accurate the result.

---

## Real-Life Example

GPS satellites use many digits of π.

A school calculation may only use:

```text
π ≈ 3.14
```

---

# 4. Degree of Accuracy in Engineering and Geometry

## Why Accuracy Matters

Since π cannot be stored exactly, calculations involve rounding.

The required accuracy depends on the application.

---

## Example 1: Drawing a Circle

Radius:

```text
5 cm
```

Using:

```text
π = 3.14
```

is usually enough.

---

## Example 2: Building a Bridge

Engineers need much higher precision.

Even tiny errors can become dangerous.

---

# Decimal Places

Decimal places refer to digits after the decimal point.

Example:

```text
3.1
```

1 decimal place

```text
3.14
```

2 decimal places

```text
3.14159
```

5 decimal places

---

# Significant Figures

Significant figures represent meaningful digits.

Example:

```text
1234
```

4 significant figures

```text
12.34
```

4 significant figures

```text
12.340
```

5 significant figures

---

## Real-Life Examples

### School Homework

```text
π = 3.14
```

usually acceptable.

---

### Aerospace Engineering

Much more precision is required.

---

### User Interface Design

Pixel measurements may require careful rounding.

Even a 1-pixel mistake can affect appearance.

# 5. Area of a Circle

## What is Area?

Area measures the space enclosed inside a circle.

```text
      *****
    **     **
   *         *
   *  AREA   *
   * INSIDE  *
    **     **
      *****
```

---

## Correct Formula

```text
Area = πr²
```

Important:

Some transcripts mistakenly state:

```text
2πr²
```

This is incorrect.

The correct formula is:

```text
πr²
```

---

## Example 1

Radius:

```text
r = 5 cm
```

Area:

```text
A = π × 5²

A = 25π

A ≈ 78.54 cm²
```

---

## Example 2

Radius:

```text
r = 10 m
```

Area:

```text
A = π × 10²

A = 100π

A ≈ 314.16 m²
```

---

## Real-Life Example

A circular garden has:

```text
Radius = 8 m
```

Area:

```text
A = π × 8²

A = 64π

≈ 201.06 m²
```

# 6. Full Rotation in Degrees

## What is a Full Rotation?

A full rotation means going completely around a circle and returning to the starting direction.

---

## Value

```text
360°
```

---

## Example

Imagine standing facing north.

Rotate:

```text
90° → East
180° → South
270° → West
360° → North
```

You return to your original direction.

---

## Quarter Turn

```text
360° ÷ 4 = 90°
```

---

## Half Turn

```text
360° ÷ 2 = 180°
```

---

## Three-Quarter Turn

```text
270°
```

---

# 7. The Concept of a Radian (and Arcs)

## What is an Arc?

An arc is a piece of the circle's boundary.

```text
      ***
    **   *
   *      *
   *      *
    *___**
```

The highlighted curved section is an arc.

---

## What is a Radian?

A radian is another way to measure angles.

Instead of using degrees, we use the relationship between:

- Arc length
- Radius

---

## Definition

When:

```text
Arc Length = Radius
```

the angle at the center equals:

```text
1 radian
```

---

## Visual Idea

```text
      *
    /   \
   /     \
  O-------*
```

If the curved arc between the two outer points has the same length as the radius:

```text
Angle = 1 radian
```

---

## Approximate Size

```text
1 radian ≈ 57.3°
```

---

# Why Radians Are Important

Radians are the natural unit used in:

- Mathematics
- Physics
- Engineering
- Computer graphics
- Machine learning

Most advanced formulas assume radians.

---

# 8. Converting Between Degrees and Radians

## Fundamental Relationship

Entire circle:

```text
360° = 2π radians
```

Half circle:

```text
180° = π radians
```

Quarter circle:

```text
90° = π/2 radians
```

---

# Degrees to Radians

Formula:

```text
Radians = Degrees × (π/180)
```

---

## Example 1

Convert:

```text
90°
```

to radians.

```text
90 × π/180

= π/2
```

Answer:

```text
π/2 radians
```

---

## Example 2

Convert:

```text
45°
```

```text
45 × π/180

= π/4
```

Answer:

```text
π/4 radians
```

---

## Example 3

Convert:

```text
60°
```

```text
60 × π/180

= π/3
```

Answer:

```text
π/3 radians
```

---

# Radians to Degrees

Formula:

```text
Degrees = Radians × (180/π)
```

---

## Example 1

Convert:

```text
π radians
```

```text
π × 180/π

= 180°
```

Answer:

```text
180°
```

---

## Example 2

Convert:

```text
π/2 radians
```

```text
(π/2) × 180/π

= 90°
```

Answer:

```text
90°
```

---

## Example 3

Convert:

```text
2π radians
```

```text
2π × 180/π

= 360°
```

Answer:

```text
360°
```
